Crayons to Canvas


My love for art began with the humble scratch of pencils and smudges of crayons in my tiny hands. My mom, a teacher, noticed early how colors seemed to dance at my command. By five, she introduced me to her school’s art teacher, Anitha ma’am, who saw a spark in me and said, “This child has magic in her fingers.” Those words lit a fire. I dove into oil pastel competitions, collecting prizes like treasures, and slowly expanded my world—sketching shadows, blending watercolors, molding clay, recycling art and even threading crochet needles. But it was portraits that stole my heart, their souls emerging under my brush, earning me recognition across states and nations.


In college, I stepped beyond the canvas, founding an art association and leading 300 volunteers to orchestrate nationwide events—chaos turned into creativity. Amid lectures, I stumbled into graphic design, teaching myself pixels and vectors until screens became my new sketchbooks. By third year, I landed a graphic design internship, a self-taught artist bridging tradition and tech. Every stroke, from crayons to digital brushes, whispers the same truth: art isn’t just what I make—it’s who I am.
